Research Summary

﹏

My research lies at the intersection of biomedical engineering, movement science, and artificial intelligence. I study how wearable systems can improve the assessment of mobility in real-world settings, and how these tools can support monitoring, neurorehabilitation, and precision health.

I am particularly interested in gait analysis, construct validity, uncertainty-aware AI, and the translation of digital mobility outcomes into research and clinical practice.

Teaching

﹏

I am committed to teaching and mentorship in both research and classroom settings, with an emphasis on clear scientific communication, quantitative reasoning, and supportive learning environments.

My teaching interests include biomechanics, neurorehabilitation, wearable technology, data analysis, and the application of engineering principles to human movement.

Service

﹏

I value academic and community service that strengthens collaboration, inclusion, and knowledge sharing across research and education.

My service interests include student mentorship, interdisciplinary initiatives, peer review, and projects that support equitable access to science and health innovation.
